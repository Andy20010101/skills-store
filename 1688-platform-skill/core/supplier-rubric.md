# Supplier Rubric

Use only visible page evidence. This rubric is intentionally conservative.

## Supplier-Level Signals

Check for:
- supplier identity clarity
- store or company naming consistency
- visible badges, years, source-manufacturer wording, or similar platform signals
- visible product breadth related to the target item
- visible trade or fulfillment hints such as MOQ, lead-time wording, sample wording, or shipping cues
- whether the supplier page appears active and populated

## Product-Level Signals

For each representative product, capture:
- product title
- visible price or price band
- MOQ if shown
- visible variants or packaging clues
- visible material, dimensions, or specification clues
- image quality and listing completeness when relevant

## Judgment Discipline

### Facts

Examples:
- visible MOQ is 200
- supplier page shows multiple related SKUs
- listing title mentions 304 stainless steel

### Heuristics

Examples:
- supplier looks more factory-like than trading-only based on listing breadth
- supplier appears to focus on the target category
- product page quality suggests higher operational maturity

### Unknowns

Examples:
- no direct proof of factory ownership
- certification status not visible
- actual production capacity unclear
- export experience unclear

## Ranking Guidance

- Prefer stronger visible evidence over stronger wording.
- Prefer suppliers with enough evidence to revisit later.
- If evidence is weak but still possibly useful, keep the candidate lower in rank and say why.
